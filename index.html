i. about

I'm a Computer Engineering student at Hacettepe University in Ankara. I got pulled into quantitative finance while studying how mathematical models can explain strategic behavior. Financial markets turned out to be the most interesting playground for that.

Right now I'm building network-based tools to map how contagion propagates through equity markets during crises. The question I keep coming back to: when a market panics, who moves first, who follows, and can you see it in the structure before it happens?

I also build things closer to hardware. My capstone is an ESP32-based drone flight controller with a custom Android remote. I like working across the stack, from embedded firmware to financial modeling.

v. projects

Strategic Impact Mapping in Financial Markets [In Progress]

An end-to-end pipeline that transforms raw S&P 500 price data into a directed strategic map of financial contagion. The framework combines asymmetric correlation modeling (A-DCC GARCH) with precision-matrix filtering and lead-follower direction assignment to identify which stocks drive market crises, through which channels, and how the network's hierarchy reorganizes under stress. Applied across ten temporal snapshots covering the 2008 GFC and 2020 COVID-19 crisis episodes.

A-DCC GARCH GLASSO PageRank Louvain Motif Analysis Granger Causality

Course: Social & Economic Networks (BBM462) Term: Spring 2026

ESP32 Flight Control System [In Progress]

A complete flight control system consisting of ESP32 firmware for real-time flight stabilization and a Kotlin/Jetpack Compose Android app for remote control via Bluetooth Low Energy. Features dual-joystick Mode 2 controls, real-time telemetry feedback, and a modular architecture separating UI, domain, and link layers. Firmware tested in QEMU emulator and validated on physical hardware. Capstone design project (BBM479/480).
